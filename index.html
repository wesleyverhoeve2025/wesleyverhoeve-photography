Wesley Verhoeve is an Amsterdam-based photographer, curator and writer.

His photography, rooted in human stories of craft and community, has been featured in the New York Times, National Geographic, and Wired.

As the founding curator of the Projected series at ICP in New York City, he organized 96 exhibitions showcasing 312 photographers from 53 countries.

He publishes Process, a weekly newsletter on photography and creative work.

Process is a weekly publication about photography and creative work, read by over 17,000 photographers and creatives in more than 145 countries.

Each issue shares thoughtful reflections and practical insights to help readers create meaningful work with clarity and intention.

Beyond the newsletter, Process hosts regular Photo Walks in cities like Amsterdam, Berlin, London, and Paris, fostering real-life connections and creative community.

Additional resources include the Process Workbook series and personalized mentorship sessions to support photographers at every stage of their projects.
